Croque Monsieur

This recipe is a slightly streamlined version of one from Andrew Rea from his YouTube channel Binging with Babish

Ingredients

  • 2 slices bread
  • 3-4 oz gruyere cheese, shredded
  • 6 slices ham
  • Bechamel
    • 1 tbsp butter, melted
    • 1 tbsp all purpose flour
    • 1 cup milk
    • Nutmeg, grated
    • Kosher Salt
    • Sprinkle white pepper - optional

Optional ingridients for a Croque Madame

  • 1 large egg
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground pepper

Directions

Bechamel

  1. In a saucepan, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until it stops spattering, the stage right before browning. Make sure to swirl regularly.
  2. At this point, add 1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour and whisk into a thick paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until the raw flour dissipates.
  3. Once the flower has dissipated, slowly add 1 cup of cold milk by adding in stages.
  4. Once the cup of milk has been added and there are no lumps in the corners, continue cooking for 5-7 minutes until nice and thick.
  5. Quickly season with grated nutmeg, a sprinkle of white pepper, and a generous pinch of kosher salt.
  6. Whisk to combine and cook for 1 minute until itâ€™s thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  7. Cover and allow to cool for 10-15 minutes before sandwich assembly.

Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 375oF
  2. Start by toasting two pieces of bread then shred 3-4 ounces of gruyere cheese.
  3. Spread a couple of tablespoons worth of the bechamel on the bottom slice of the bread, just enough to cover it.
  4. Top that with a generous amount of gruyere cheese along with 6 slices of ham, topping with another generous amount of gruyere, spreading the bechamel on the top slice of bread before placing on top.
  5. Before toasting, spread another layer of bechamel on top of the sandwich and another layer of gruyere cheese.
  6. Place into a 375oF oven until thoroughly brown and bubbly.
  7. For a Croque Madame top with a fried sunny side up egg seasoned with kosher salt and freshly ground pepper.
